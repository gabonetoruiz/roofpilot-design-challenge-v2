RoofPilot · Product Designer challenge

Job page — mobile redesign, handoff notes

Gabriel Ruiz · info@gabrielruiz.us

Scope: the brief mentioned both mobile and desktop, but the email that assigned this challenge said the mobile version is what actually gets reviewed — so every hour went into that one surface. The desktop pass called for in the written brief was intentionally left out to protect the quality of the mobile screen.

What changed, and why

An always-visible "next step"

The original screen never said what to do next once it opened. A dedicated card at the top turns whatever's most urgent on the job — an unanswered estimate, a missing signature — into one sentence and one button, so a rep knows the move before they've even scrolled.

Stage progress as a segmented bar, not a 6-dot stepper

An early version used a dot-and-line stepper across all 6 pipeline stages, but it got cramped and hard to scan once every stage needed a label. It's now a compact tag ("2 OF 6 · ESTIMATING") plus a row of color-coded segments — green for done, orange for the current stage, gray for what's ahead. Same information, readable at a glance, and it scales cleanly if RoofPilot ever adds a stage.

The four daily actions live in a floating bottom bar

Note, Task, Email and Message are reachable from anywhere on the page with one thumb. The higher-stakes actions the brief flagged as hardest to find — estimate, proposal, invoice, work order — get their own "Create" button, raised in the center of the same bar with a glass/blur treatment so it reads as the primary action without competing with the four daily ones. Locked items (invoice, work order) stay visible but disabled with a one-line reason, instead of disappearing.

Every action confirms itself three ways

A toast for the instant, an immediate update to the relevant card (a new contact appears in the list right away), and a permanent line in the Activity timeline underneath. That's a direct answer to "you're not sure if it worked" — the toast fades, but the card and the timeline are proof that stays.

Job fields are read-only by default, editable on demand

The original screen showed empty dropdowns and date pickers even for fields nobody had filled in yet. The redesign shows clean label/value rows, and one Edit tap turns the whole group into inputs with a single Save — not ten separate fields each needing their own confirmation.

Contacts: adding, and now also removing, with one Primary enforced

This is the flow demoed below. Adding a second contact was the brief's explicit ask; while building it out further, two related gaps became obvious and got fixed too: contacts can now be removed (blocked once only one is left, since a job always needs at least one contact), and checking "Set as primary contact" on a new contact automatically transfers the Primary tag away from the old one — there's only ever one Primary contact at a time, and if that contact gets deleted, the next remaining one is promoted automatically so the job is never left without one.

Notes got their own list, separate from Activity

Notes were originally only visible as entries in the Activity feed — readable once, but not something you could revisit, fix a typo in, or remove. There's now a dedicated Notes card with create, edit-in-place, and delete, while Activity stays what it should be: a permanent, read-only audit trail. New notes still log an Activity entry, same as before.

Visual direction matched to RoofPilot's real product

Colors, card shapes, badges and spacing are pulled from RoofPilot's actual dashboard and job board at roofpilot.ai, not a generic SaaS style — white rounded cards on a light gray page, orange as the one brand/CTA color, blue for informational actions, green for positive states, pill-shaped status badges. Every icon comes from a single set (Hugeicons, stroke style) instead of mixed icon sources.

This build: compiled as a component, not a static file

This final version was rebuilt in Claude Design as a self-contained component (state + logic separated from the template, matching the same interactions and data as the working prototype) rather than hand-edited HTML/CSS/JS. Same design decisions above, same behavior — just packaged for a cleaner handoff.
